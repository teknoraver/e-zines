 Û    Subject : BBSs vs. The Net                                              Û
 Û                                                                            Û
 ÛÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÜÛ


 ÛßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßßÛ
 Û                                                                            Û
 Û      Being a BBS SysOp used to be something to envy.  Not everyone could   Û
 Û  afford to have a BBS, the costs were very high.  More recently, BBSs      Û
 Û  have become virtually commonplace.  The BBS scene has flourished,         Û
 Û  competition has been on the rise, and boards have gone up in quality.     Û
 Û  The top boards now consist of 15+ nodes, all pushing at 28.8 kbps or      Û
 Û  even 33.6 kbps for the lucky ones.  With the advent of the Internet,      Û
 Û  though, we have been presented with a new form of data transfer, FTP      Û
 Û  sites.  FTP sites now run on super fast internet connections capable of   Û
 Û  over 100k/s, assuming perfect efficiency.  For a scene that relies        Û
 Û  purely on speed, these new holding grounds are obviously the ideal        Û
 Û  choice.  There are, however, several issues that need to be tackled       Û
 Û  before we can assume that the warez scene should move to sites.           Û
 Û                                                                            Û
 Û      A fast BBS used to be the main form of "declaring a release."  If a   Û
 Û  group released a program and another group released it 30 minutes later,  Û
 Û  the seniors could tell which group got to it first by seeing when the     Û
 Û  release started to get on boards, or, more appropriately, when it got on  Û
 Û  the competition's World Headquarter.  I, personally, would refine that    Û
 Û  to say that a release has been declared as soon as it has gotten on a     Û
 Û  headquarter of a major couriering group, since it is the couriers that    Û
 Û  are responsible for pushing the warez.  The dilemna arrives when a        Û
 Û  group tries to claim a release that they put only on FTP sites and on     Û
 Û  IRC.  If the group's couriers aren't fast enough, or if the release was   Û
 Û  lost by only a few minutes, it may not have gotten on any BBSs yet.  Is   Û
 Û  an FTP site a proper "declaration" site?  Some would argue.  I,           Û
 Û  personally, think that if the site is an official headquarter site of at  Û
 Û  least one major group, it should be.  FTP sites are very handy for        Û
 Û  keeping track of things.  They keep track of everything!  Not only do     Û
 Û  they tell you what date the release was uploaded on, but the time too,    Û
 Û  down to the nearest second!                                               Û
 Û                                                                            Û
 Û      I think that it is evident that FTP sites are a valid form of         Û
 Û  stating a release, as long as they are big enough, which is the same      Û
 Û  requirement we would put on a board.  It is not enough to upload a        Û
 Û  release to a single-node board in Afghanastan and say that you have       Û
 Û  released it, nor is it enough to upload the release to a 3k/s anonymous   Û
 Û  FTP site.                                                                 Û
 Û                                                                            Û
 Û      Another argument that people often make about the Internet as a       Û
 Û  releasing grounds is that IRC is a perfectly good place to declare a      Û
 Û  release.  This, I would have to disprove of.  To my knowledge, IRC does   Û
 Û  not keep a log of any times that anything was offered on or anything to   Û
 Û  that degree.  Moreover, IRC involves pure word of mouth, never anything   Û
 Û  definite, so if DOD tries to release Nashville and PWA tries to do the    Û
 Û  same on the same day, nearly at the same time, and the groups are too     Û
 Û  dumb (note, this would NEVER happen with such experienced groups, these   Û
 Û  guys know what to do if they have such a sweet release!) to upload the    Û
 Û  release to any sites or to any boards, nobody will ever know who really   Û
 Û  released it first.                                                        Û
 Û                                                                            Û
 Û      Ultimately, we wouldn't take the word of a small BBS to tell us when  Û
 Û  a release was done, nor would we take the word of a small FTP site.  We   Û
 Û  will not take the word of IRC to tell us when a release was done, as we   Û
 Û  are all a bunch of lying schmucks and would never get anywhere doing      Û
 Û  that :-).  Those are my two cents, if you have any comments or            Û
 Û  suggestions, please write in!                                             Û
