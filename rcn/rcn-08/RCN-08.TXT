 Û      The scene began as a form of getting software without having to pay   Û
 Û  for it.  Ultimately this was for the benefit of the end user, perhaps     Û
 Û  you.  It was taken for granted that if the end user doesn't pay for the   Û

 Û  releases via store suppliers, who would upload them to a supplier HQ FTP  Û
 Û  site, where the group seniors would grab it, test it, crack it, package   Û

 Û  charging their member boards and distribution sites a fee for the         Û
 Û  assigning of a courier to their location, and oftentimes not even that.   Û
 Û  So far I understand that Genesis, Razor 1911, Hybrid, and TDU-JAM have    Û
 Û  paid their suppliers and charged their boards to make up for the cost.    Û

 Û  believe in a common goal that we are here to help the scene by giving     Û
 Û  away FREE games.  If our suppliers are unable to attain software without  Û
 Û  paying for it, they will be responsible for the funds, as the problem     Û
 Û  should end at their level.                                                Û
 Û                                                                            Û
 Û      The whole cycle, from the supplier to the board to the end user,      Û
 Û  should not result in the transfer of any money between the groups, the    Û
 Û  boards, and the suppliers.  We like to tell ourselves that the scene is   Û
 Û  just a game and we're all here to have fun, but games never involve       Û
