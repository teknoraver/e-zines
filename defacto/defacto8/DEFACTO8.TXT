 Doomsday@ix.netcom.com. All comments, flames, etc will be answered as soon

 Ok before you even jump into hacking a system, or even connect to one
 you need a # to connect to. (Sorry there is no IP Address scanning.) The only
 way to find #s that are carriers is by dialing up each # manually and
 listening for a connect noise. Fortunately, people wrote programs to make the
 computer do all of that plus much much more. Programs like Blue Beep and Tone
 Loc are the best that i've found. I reccommend Tone Loc for scanning, it has
 full logs, carrier logs, and mapping of a prefix, etc. Blue Beep is more of a
 all around phreaking program than Tone Loc.

 First of all, you need to figure out what #s you are going to scan.
 There are 10,000 numbers in a prefix, from say 818-705-XXXX. A prefix is the
 3 numbers after the area code. To scan a whole prefix it usually takes 3
 days non-stop or about 7 days if you do it part time. I reccommend either
 getting a dedicated computer with a shitty modem, or scan while you sleep.
 I can't really go into detail about how to run or setup the program, READ THE
 DOCS is all i can say. Once everyday or a couple of hours you should check
 your connect.log or whatever you saved it to and see if you hit the jackpot.
 When the program connects, it trys a series of characters or comamnds to
 make the log more informative.

 After you found carriers, logon to them and try to figure out what
 they are. Usually people are interested with System 75s and System 85s
 so you can make PBXs. PBXs are used by companies so their employees can
 bill to one statement. They are used by people like us to make free phone
 calls (=]). Why are PBXs the only ones people want? They don't have ANI
 like 950s now have (a popular way of phreaking many years ago). And now,
 cellular hacking is the big thing.

 But anyways, after you find out what logon it is, by checking local
 hack BBSes, the web, alt.2600, or irc, i get the defaults for the system
 first. Defaults are the passwords that the OS comes built in when you install
 it. Nowadays I don't think the defaults still work but there are always
 retarded sysadministrators that forget to change the default passwords. If
 the defaults don't work you are going to get a program like crackerjack and
 crack them yourself. Thats a bit involved to teach, so I won't go into it for
 now.

 Ok, you've made it onto a system. You are here, hopefully with a
 system admin's account. Enjoy fooling around with systems, maybe if you're
 lucky you cracked into a bank or the DMV. (Yea right!)

  P.S. Some places have made scanning illegal! Even though you are calling a
  number once and only once! Find out about your area before you do it!
